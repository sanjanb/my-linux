thought:thought:Converting completely to Linux means "nuking" your Windows partition and giving the entire SSD to CachyOS. On an ROG Strix, this is actually a great move for AI/ML performance because you remove all the background "bloat" of Windows 11, freeing up that massive 13GB of RAM you saw earlier.

Here is the "Full Conversion" path:

### 1. The Strategy: "Nuke and Pave"

When you reach the **Partitions** screen in the CachyOS installer:

* **Instead of "Manual Partitioning":** Select **"Erase Disk"**.
* **File System:** Choose **BTRFS**.
* **Swap:** Choose **"Swap to File"** (8GB or 16GB is good for your RAM size).
* **Encryption:** You can choose to encrypt the entire drive here (LUKS). Since you're removing BitLocker, this is the Linux equivalent for security.

### 2. What you lose (The "Cons")

* **Armoury Crate:** You lose the official ASUS software. However, we've already replaced it with `asusctl` and `supergfxctl`, which are actually *better* and lighter on Linux.
* **Windows-only AI Tools:** If you use specific proprietary software (like NVIDIA Broadcast for noise removal), those don't have direct Linux equivalents, though `noisetorch` or `easyeffects` come close.
* **Firmware Updates:** ASUS pushes BIOS updates through Windows. **Recommendation:** Before you delete Windows, go to the MyASUS app and ensure your BIOS is updated to the latest version. On Linux, you'll have to update BIOS via a USB stick in the future.

### 3. Checklist for "Full Conversion"

* **[ ] Browser Sync:** Ensure Chrome/Firefox sync is on so your bookmarks and history move over.
* **[ ] Windows Folders:** Back up your `Documents`, `Pictures`, and `Downloads` to your D-Drive or the cloud.
* **[ ] The "Rescue" USB:** Always keep your Windows 11 Bootable USB (made with Media Creation Tool) in a drawer. If you ever need to sell the laptop or go back for work, you'll need it.

---

### 🛠️ Final Installer settings for "Full Linux"

If you go this route, choose these in the CachyOS installer:

1. **Bootloader:** `systemd-boot` or `GRUB`.
2. **Kernel:** `linux-cachyos` (standard) or `linux-cachyos-lto` (if you want every drop of performance).
3. **Optimized Repos:** The installer will detect your **x86-64-v3** i7-13650HX automatically. This will give you a system that is roughly **10-15% faster** than standard Arch Linux.

### My Recommendation

Since you are an **AI Intern**, a full conversion is a "power move." Most high-end AI research happens on Linux. Being comfortable in a full-Linux environment will make you a much more valuable developer.
