Hyprland is famous for how it handles multi-monitor workspaces—it treats each screen as an independent workspace "view," allowing for a much more flexible workflow than Windows.

However, because you are using an **NVIDIA 40-series GPU**, you need to add a few specific lines to your config to ensure everything is buttery smooth and free of flickering.

---

### 🖥️ 1. Configuring your Monitors

Hyprland does not use a GUI for monitor settings by default. You define them in your `~/.config/hypr/hyprland.conf` file.

**The Syntax:**
`monitor = name, resolution, position, scale`

**Example Setup for your ROG Strix + External Monitor:**

```bash
# 1. Your Laptop Screen (Centered)
# Resolution: 2560x1600, Refresh: 240Hz, Position: 0x0
monitor = eDP-1, 2560x1600@240, 0x0, 1.25

# 2. Your External Monitor (Placed to the Left)
# Resolution: 1920x1080, Position: -1920x0 (Left of the main screen)
monitor = HDMI-A-1, 1920x1080@144, -1920x0, 1

```

* **Pro Tip:** If you don't know your monitor's name (like `eDP-1`), run `hyprctl monitors` in your terminal to see a list of connected screens.

---

### 🛠️ 2. Essential NVIDIA Fixes for Multi-Monitor

NVIDIA on Wayland can sometimes show "flickering" on secondary screens. To fix this in 2026, ensure these are in your `hyprland.conf`:

```bash
# Force explicit sync (fixes most NVIDIA flickering)
env = NVD_BACKEND,direct

# Ensure the UI uses the right DRM device
env = AQ_DRM_DEVICES,/dev/dri/card0:/dev/dri/card1

```

---

### 🎨 3. Multi-Monitor in HyDE

Since you are using **HyDE**, it will automatically try to place a **Waybar** (taskbar) on every monitor.

* **Workspaces:** By default, HyDE will show Workspaces 1-5 on Monitor 1 and 6-10 on Monitor 2.
* **Wallpaper:** When you use the HyDE wallpaper selector (`Super + Shift + W`), it will intelligently span or mirror your wallpaper across both screens depending on the theme you've chosen.

---

### 💡 Multi-Monitor Pro-Tips

* **Workspace Binding:** You can force a specific workspace to always open on a specific monitor.
* `workspace = 3, monitor:HDMI-A-1` (This ensures your AI training terminal always stays on your big screen).


* **Focus Follows Mouse:** In Hyprland, you don't need to click a monitor to use it; just move your mouse over, and that screen becomes active.
* **Gaming:** If you game on the external monitor, use the `supergfxctl -m Hybrid` mode to ensure the game uses the NVIDIA card while the laptop screen handles the Discord/Chrome windows via the Intel chip.

### 🛠️ Professional "Display Profiles"

If you want to manually switch between "Gaming Mode" (Single Screen) and "AI Work Mode" (Dual Screen), you can add these keybinds to your HyDE config:

```bash
# In ~/.config/hypr/hyprland.conf

# Press Super + Alt + G for Gaming (External Only, Max Performance)
bind = $mainMod ALT, G, exec, hyprctl keyword monitor "eDP-1, disable" && hyprctl keyword monitor "HDMI-A-1, preferred, 0x0, 1"

# Press Super + Alt + W for Work (Dual Monitor)
bind = $mainMod ALT, W, exec, hyprctl keyword monitor "eDP-1, preferred, 0x0, 1.25" && hyprctl keyword monitor "HDMI-A-1, preferred, -1920x0, 1"

```

### 🎨 Why this is great for your setup:

1. **NVIDIA Stability:** By using `hyprctl keyword monitor`, you are talking directly to the compositor. This is much more stable on NVIDIA 40-series cards than using external tools like `arandr`.
2. **Productivity:** You don't have to open "Settings" every time you sit at your desk. You plug in one cable, and your entire workspace (Waybar, Wallpapers, and Windows) shifts to your dual-monitor layout.
3. **Efficiency:** When you unplug to go to a meeting, your laptop screen immediately becomes the primary, and your windows will "snap" back to it automatically.
